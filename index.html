Adopt an animal that lives in the rainforest

Choose from a variety of different animals from the rainforests. Get weekly updates and learn more about your animal. The money goes to saving the rainforests, providing your animal and it’s family a better place to live, as well as it helps slowing down the climate change.

Rainforests play an important role in sustaining life. Sadly, every year large portions of them are cut down for logging, mining and cattle ranches. About 17 precent of the Amazonian rainforest has been destroyed over the past 50 years.

When we loose rainforests, we also loose a valuable natural resource. Tropical rainforests are centers of biodiversity, holding an estimated half of the world’s plants and animals. Rainforests also produce, store and filter water, protecting against soli erosion, floods and drought.

Rainforests affect climate change with the ability to absorbe carbon. The deforestation on the other hand, drives the global warming further. When a tree is cut down, all the carbon that the forest were storing, is released into the atmosphere. Keeping forests intact should therefore be a priority In the fight against climate change.
